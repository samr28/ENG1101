#BlackLivesMatter

Trayvon Martin was an African American teenager living in Florida. He was a really good kid. When he was only nine years old, he saved his father’s life by pulling him out of a fire in their apartment. He also worked cutting grass, washing cars, and babysitting to earn his own money. Trayvon played on his high school football team and his coach said that he was one of the best players on the team. As you can see, he was a pretty typical high schooler. In 2009 he enrolled in a seven week program which introduced him to aviation. This got him interested in planes and how they work. This passion eventually led him to want to work on planes. As he prepared to head off to college, he put his football aspirations aside in favor of a career in aviation.

Unfortunately, during Trayvon’s junior year of high school, he got involved with marijuana and ended up getting suspended from school. One night, he went out to a 7-11 store to get some Skittles and drinks. As he was returning from the store, George Zimmerman, a volunteer neighborhood watch, spotted Trayvon and called the police saying that he appeared “suspicious.” After ending the call, a violent encounter took place between Zimmerman and Trayvon. It ended with Zimmerman fatally shooting Trayvon.

When the police arrived on the scene, they attempted to revive Trayvon but were unsuccessful. They took Zimmerman in to the police station for questioning. He was questioned for five hours and eventually released. The police chief stated that there was no evidence pointing to anything other than self defense.

Once the public heard of this case, they were outraged. Trayvon had done nothing wrong. He was shot and killed because of his skin color. There were protests staged around the U.S. calling for Zimmerman to be arrested. Trayvon’s mom created a change.org petition which garnered over 2.2 million signatures. This eventually led to Zimmerman being tried and convicted of murder.

After Zimmerman was convicted, posts on social media started showing up with the hashtag “#BlackLivesMatter.” The three key organizers were Alicia Garza, Patrisse Cullors, and Opal Tometi. They were disgusted with what happened to Trayvon and wanted to work to make sure that this doesn’t happen to any other young black men. In order to prevent this from happening again, they started the Black Lives Matter movement. The movement aims to shed light on the injustices facing African Americans.

The movement really took off in 2014 after two African American males were killed by police officers. They protested around the U.S. and called for action. These protests were generally ones that made people so uncomfortable that they felt that they must address the issue. One of these tactics called a “die-in” is where protesters lie on the ground and simulate being dead. These protests ultimately resulted in raised awareness for the issues that African Americans are facing in America.

Change.org petition

Weeks after the shooting of Trayvon, Tracy Martin (his mom) created a change.org petition with the goal of getting Angela Corey, Florida’s 4th DIstrict State’s Attorney to investigate the case. Passionate followers shared the petition on social media sites like Facebook and Twitter. This resulted in the petition receiving 2,267,079 signatures in the span of a few months. Eventually, Angela Corey tried Zimmerman with the charge of second-degree murder. The trial started with opening statements on June 24, 2013. The jury started deliberations on July 12th and on the 13th, the jury found him not guilty.

This left supporters with a bad feeling about the American judicial system. Ultimately this discomfort only strengthened the Black Lives Matter movement.

Second degree murder: Intentional murder without planning or a killing caused by the offender’s lack of concern for human life.

Aretha Franklin

On August 16th, Aretha Franklin, a singer songwriter passed away. She was also widely known as the Queen of Soul. Most people are familiar with her music but aren’t aware of the civil rights activism that she has participated in. She provided monetary donations to many civil rights groups. She would also perform at various benefits and protests to help raise awareness. Some of her songs are even based on this activism work that she’s done. For example, “Respect” and “Natural Woman” have become anthems for social change.

When she passed away, the official Black Lives Matter facebook posted about her passing. The post talks about how they appreciate her sharing her brilliance and helping so many people throughout her life. This shows that the community can stay strong through difficult times with things like music.

Blue Lives Matter

Another very similar movement called Blue Lives Matter was also formed around this time. The organizers likely used the same naming structure in order to piggyback on the success of the Black Lives Matter movement.

The Blue Lives Matter movement is similar to the Black Lives Matter but instead of focusing on the lives of African Americans, it focuses on the lives of police officers. While this may seem like the polar opposite, it isn’t. Both movements can coexist. Both movements aim to make our world safer. They just focus on two different groups of people. Ideally, we should make the world safer for all people. In fact, there is another movement called All Lives Matter with this exact philosophy. Many argue that this movement is disrespectful and reflects a view of dismissal for the racial issues that we are facing in this country. All of these movements are important for furthering social equality and hopefully one day, we will reach our goals and everyone will be treated equally.

Darian's music video

A post by Facebook user “Darian” was made on December 5th, 2014. In this post, he shared a rap music video that talked about systemic racism and police brutality. In the video, they say “it’s a shame that I’m afraid to see my nephew grow up and praying that he won't be next.” This is of course a reference to police brutality and the killing of innocent people.

Later in the song, she starts listing off black men that were killed by police officers for no reason. “Michael Brown, Eric Garner, Trayvon, and next month it will probably be a rest in peace Dayshawn, or Kevin, or Don....” By listing off all of these names, she is trying to show how out of control things are getting. She also counts on her fingers and eventually runs out of fingers which again proves the point that things are getting out of control.

By presenting this information in a catchy music video, the author is able to convey their ideas and keep the audience's attention more easily. It ends up being very effective.

Eric Garner

In 2014, a black man named Eric Garner was killed by police officers in New York City. A police officer put him in a chokehold while arresting him. Chokeholds are prohibited by the NYPD so this officer shouldn’t have even been doing this in the first place. Eric repeated “I can’t breathe” eleven times while lying facedown on the sidewalk. Eleven times. He then lost consciousness and the officers left him laying on the sidewalk. Seven minutes later, an ambulance arrived. The medics didn’t perform CPR on Eric at the scene and he was pronounced dead at the hospital an hour later.

Keith Kuder made a post on Facebook with a video of a comedian talking about the incident. He basically made fun of the officers saying that they couldn’t follow basic rules. He then briefly touches on systemic racism saying that the officers can check a book to see what’s allowed and in this book, it says that killing black people is ok. We all know that this is not true but it helped make his systemic racism point clear.
