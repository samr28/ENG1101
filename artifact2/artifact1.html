#ALSIceBucketChallenge

To understand the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge, we first need to understand what ALS is. Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (also called Lou Gehrig's disease) is a non-curable disease where nerve cells break down resulting in loss of muscle functions. This leads to patients losing control of their muscles. The disease starts with subtle fine motor skill issues and/or muscle twitching. As the disease progresses, patients lose control of more muscles including those that allow for talking, walking, and breathing. At this point, they will need outside help for basic tasks such as eating, getting dressed, or showering. The disease affects everyone differently so it’s impossible to tell how fast one’s condition will worsen and which muscles will stop working when.

Unfortunately, ALS is always fatal because it will eventually make the patient lose control of their diaphragm making it impossible to breathe without the help of an external machine.

Facts

  • Currently a little over 5.000 people in the US are diagnosed with ALS every year (15 new per day.)
  • Military veterans are more likely to contract ALS
  • ALS is not contagious

The ALS Ice Bucket Challenge is an activity where the participant dumps a large bucket of ice water on their head. It’s usually more popular in the summer and warmer parts of the year. The challenge went viral during the summer of 2014. Participants would dump the water on their head and then typically nominate three other people to do the same. Those nominees then have 24 hours to complete the challenge. If they don’t then they have to make a donation to the ALS Association.

The challenge was first shown on television in 2014 when hosts of the Golf Channel performed a live on-air ice bucket challenge. Later that year, Matt Lauer did the challenge on NBC’s The Today show. On that same day, a golfer named Chris Kennedy did the challenge and challenged his cousin Jeanette Serchia. Her husband had ALS for 11 years and this challenge was the first documented case connected to ALS. At this point the challenge wasn’t directly connected to ALS, those challenged that chose not to participate would simply donate to a charity of their choice.

A man named Pat Quinn (diagnosed with ALS in 2013) was friends on Facebook with Jeanette Serchia. After seeing her post about the challenge, he encouraged his friends to take the challenge. Pete Frates (diagnosed with ALS in 2012) then saw these posts and began posting and sharing with his friends. These two are credited with starting the challenge. It soon went viral, reaching as high as the president, Barack Obama.

The challenge was a huge success, resulting in more than 2.4 million posts on Facebook. In terms of monetary donations, the ALS Association reported that they had received $41.8 million in donations from 739,000 new donors.

With this money, the ALS Association made a huge research breakthrough. In 2016, researchers using the donation money discovered that the disease was connected to a certain gene which may eventually lead to new treatment possibilities. The gene, NEK1, is associated with 3% of ALS cases but is present in both inherited and sporadic forms of ALS which provides a new target for treatments.

Mark Zuckerberg

In 2015, Mark Zuckerberg was challenged by Governor Chris Christie to do the ice bucket challenge. He then nominated Bill Gates, Sheryl Sandberg, and Reed Hastings.

The video starts off with Mark in what looks like his backyard. He talks about the challenge and how it works. Since he was nominated early in the challenge, he spent some of his time explaining how it works for those that hadn't seen it before.

He then dumps a large bucket of ice water on his head and doesn’t have much of a reaction to it. He just says “wow, that was really cold.” Most of the other participants yell and have a much stronger reaction to a huge bucket of ice cold water being dumped on their head.

There’s a running joke that says that Mark is a robot. We all know that this isn’t true but some of his actions (like his reaction to the ice water) make it seem as if it were true.

Elon Musk

On August 16th, Elon Musk participated in the ice bucket challenge. As the founder of multiple hugely successful technology startups, he is a very creative person. So for his challenge, he decided to do something extraordinary. He built a contraption with five separate buckets hung on a tree. Five buckets, one for each of his children. The kids all pulled their strings at the same time and the buckets all dumped on Elon.

His challenge video was unique because it was shot from a far distance and you couldn’t really hear what (if anything) he was saying. He didn’t nominate anyone in the video and instead opted to write it in the description. In addition, the nominations were made by Elon’s sons instead of him. Elon decided to nominate Minecraft creator Markus Peterson, Johnny Depp, and Tonk Hawk. His unlisted video received half a million views and was posted directly to the SpaceX Youtube channel.

Bill Gates

On August 15th, 2015, Bill Gates, the founder of Microsoft, joined in and participated in the ice bucket challenge. He was challenged by Mark Zuckerberg and chose to nominate Elon Musk, Ryan Seacrest, and Chris Anderson.

The video starts off with Bill watching Mark do the ice bucket challenge on a new Microsoft Surface device. He then says “I’m glad to give to ALS, it’s a great cause but I want to do it better than it’s been done.” He then shows designs for a contraption that will dump the ice bucket on his head when he pulls a rope.

His video was particularly interesting because instead of simply pouring a bucket of ice water over his head, he built this machine that did it for him. This shows his internal creativity and extreme excitement for engineering and design. This personal touch made his video stand out and was part of the reason that this video received over 26 million views on Youtube.

Markus Persson

Markus is a Swedish programmer and video game designer. He is best known for starting the video game company Mojang which in turn created the popular sandbox building game, Minecraft.

In his video, Markus attached a camera to his ice bucket. By doing this, his video gained a unique perspective. It looks like he had a lot of fun recording the video. He chose to nominate some other video game designers like Cliff Bleszinski, Bobby Kotick, and Phil Spencer.

Phil Spencer

Phil Spencer is the current vice-president of gaming at Microsoft. He is also the head of the Xbox brand, leading many different teams responsible for gaming at Microsoft. In his ice bucket challenge, Phil is wearing an Aperture Laboratories shirt which is from a popular video game called Portal. This shows his inner video game loving self and makes him seem very relatable and down to earth.

Two Microsoft employees then pour a huge bucket of ice water onto his head. The video goes into slow motion to add dramatic effect. This works really well and helps the viewer feel how cold the water actually is. We can see ice flying out in slow motion and this really adds a unique spin to the typical ice bucket challenge video. Phil then says that he nominates Shuhei Yoshida from Sony, Reggie Fils-Aime from Nintendo and Gabe Newell from Valve to participate in the challenge.
