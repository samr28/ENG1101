#MeToo

Interactive! hover over italic words to see the definition

In October of 2017, film producer Harvey Weinstein was accused of sexual abuse . Dozens of women accused him of rape, sexual assault , and sexual abuse . He was able to get away with this for some time because of his use of the “casting couch” technique. This is where a producer will exchange casting roles for sexual favors. Unfortunately, this is somewhat common in the industry. Of course this is completely immoral and most will agree that it shouldn’t be allowed. One of the women affected by this said “if Harvey Weinstein invites you to a private party at the four seasons, don’t go.”

When the media tried to get first hand accounts of those affected, none of the women would speak on record. The editors decided that they couldn’t mention the assault allegations without the victim's input.

In 2015, Weinstein was taken in by the police for questioning after Ambra Gutierrez, an Italian model, accused him of sexual assault . Ambra worked with police to get an audio where Weinstein admitted to these acts. When the police records and case information went public, many media sites published negative stories about Ambra, calling her an opportunist. Some publications even agreed to help suppress the allegations. The district attorney ultimately decided not to file charges, citing that they didn’t have enough evidence.


Weinstein even admitted in an interview “yes, I did offer them [girls] acting jobs in exchange for sex, but so did and still does everyone.” So there was clearly enough evidence yet the attorney dropped the case. This shows that our current system is not set up to properly handle sexual misconduct cases.

In order to combat this and attempt to fix our broken system, a movement called “Me Too” was founded. This movement was originally started by a social activist named Tarana Burke. She started using “#MeToo” on Myspace as part of a campaign to promote female empowerment among women of color. That first movement wasn’t widely successful but was revived in 2017 by actress Alyssa Milano. She used the hashtag to draw attention to sexual assault and harassment.

This time, the campaign was hugely successful. Within 24 hours, there had been 12 million posts on Facebook using the hashtag. Facebook reported that 45% of U.S. users had at least one friend that used the hashtag in a post.

The campaign resulted in a congressional bill. This bill proposed member and employee training on the Congress Act. The bill was bipartisan, hoping to gain support from both sides of the aisle. Specifically, the bill aimed to change how the legislative branch treats sexual harassment complaints. The old system was slow and very wasteful of federal resources. In fact, it was reported that in the past ten years, $15 million dollars had been spent settling these complaints. This bill ensures that claims will be settled in 180 days or less and the funding would come from the involved Senators and Representatives. The settlements will also become public, instead of remaining a secret. In addition, these rights would now be given to interns, fellows, and other unpaid interns.

Trump mocking movement

On October 2nd, the Washington Post posted a video of president Donald Trump mocking the me too movement. He was speaking at a rally in Mississippi and mocked a woman that accused Brett Kavanaugh of sexual assault . Trump tried to claim that the Me Too movement was unfairly hurting men.

Trump tried to claim that she wasn’t remembering things properly. He did an impersonation of the woman saying “I don’t know,” “upstairs? Downstairs? Where was it?” “I don’t know.” This could be a huge blow to the movement as the President has a huge social media following. This means that a lot of people will see his speech and his opinion will be heard. This could eventually lead to the Me Too movement losing some followers and sexual assault continuing to be an issue in America.

Rose Mcgowan

In a Twitter post by Rose Mcgowan, she talks about how some people are dismissing the Me Too movement. She says that she’s tired of posts that don’t really mean anything. These posts are gathering together and drawing attention to the issue but not actually solving the problem.

The comments on the post talk about the origins of the Me Too movement. One person posted about Tarana Burke. Tarana is a civil rights activist and stated the original (less successful) Me Too movement in 2006. This movement has the same purpose at the second one: to raise awareness of sexual assault . The movement was revived by Alyssa Milano in 2017.

It seems that the people in the comments are getting caught up in who started the movement and who should really get the credit for it. Instead of focusing on this, they should be looking at what the movement can accomplish.

The only time men care about sexual harassment

A twitter user named PMbeers posted on twitter just a few days ago that “The only time white men care about sexual harassment... is when they think a man of color did it.” The post shows how the Me Too movement transcends through so many different social issues like race. It also talks about the patriarchy implying that white men are the ones making decisions that affect all of us. This is not true but the post is trying to paint a picture much worse than it actually is. While the intention of the post is clear, it doesn’t take into account many other factors which are at play like socioeconomic status.

In the comments, there is a Twitter user who tried to nullify the point by claiming that they are a white male concerned with rape of anyone, of any sex or color. So he is providing anecdotal evidence of the case being false. Of course there were replies to this comment and one of which was “Awesome, Go out and share your views with other men.”

Sexual assault in India

This post talks about sexual assault survivors in India and how that environment makes it very difficult to deal with sexual assault. They don’t have the same resources available to them as we do here in America. As the movement is becoming more widespread people are struggling to define what constitutes Gatessexual harassment.

In these different cultures, people are more afraid to speak out because it could be considered shameful and they won’t get the support that they need. This is particularly relevant in today’s political environment where we are getting close to electing people who may have committed these things.

In the United States, we think that sexual assault is something that is isolated here but this post helps us realize that this is a global issue. In fact, it’s more of an issue in other places than it is here in the States because of cultural differences and stigmas.

Meme mocking the movement

In this post, a Twitter user made a post that mocked the Me Too movement. The image that they posted was a spin off of what the kids like to call “memes.” In this meme, a lady is crying and it has a caption saying that her doctor molested her when she was born. Of course this is not actually molestation. The meme is trying to say that sexual assault is not real and that those that have experienced it are making a deal out of nothing. This is not true and really hurting the Me Too movement.

The post is really trying to make light of a very serious issue. This may just be a way of coping but that seems somewhat unlikely.
