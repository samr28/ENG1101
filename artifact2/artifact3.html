#BlackLivesMatter

In October of 2017, film producer Harvey Weinstein was accused of sexual abuse. Dozens of women accused him of rape, sexual assault, and sexual abuse. He was able to get away with this for some time because of his use of the “casting couch” technique. This is where a producer will exchange casting roles for sexual favors. Unfortunately, this is somewhat common in the industry. Of course this is completely immoral and most will agree that it shouldn’t be allowed. One of the women affected by this said “if Harvey Weinstein invites you to a private party at the four seasons, don’t go.”

When the media tried to get first hand accounts of those affected, none of the women would speak on record. The editors decided that they couldn’t mention the assault allegations without the victim's input.

In 2015, Weinstein was taken in by the police for questioning after Ambra Gutierrez, an Italian model, accused him of sexual assault. Ambra worked with police to get an audio where Weinstein admitted to these acts. When the police records and case information went public, many media sites published negative stories about Ambra, calling her an opportunist. Some publications even agreed to help suppress the allegations. The district attorney ultimately decided not to file charges, citing that they didn’t have enough evidence.

Weinstein even admitted in an interview “yes, I did offer them [girls] acting jobs in exchange for sex, but so did and still does everyone.” So there was clearly enough evidence yet the attorney dropped the case. This shows that our current system is not set up to properly handle sexual misconduct cases.

In order to combat this and attempt to fix our broken system, a movement called “Me Too” was founded. This movement was originally started by a social activist named Tarana Burke. She started using “#MeToo” on Myspace as part of a campaign to promote female empowerment among women of color. That first movement wasn’t widely successful but was revived in 2017 by actress Alyssa Milano. She used the hashtag to draw attention to sexual assault and harassment.

This time, the campaign was hugely successful. Within 24 hours, there had been 12 million posts on Facebook using the hashtag. Facebook reported that 45% of U.S. users had at least one friend that used the hashtag in a post.

The campaign resulted in a congressional bill. This bill proposed member and employee training on the Congress Act. The bill was bipartisan, hoping to gain support from both sides of the aisle. Specifically, the bill aimed to change how the legislative branch treats sexual harassment complaints. The old system was slow and very wasteful of federal resources. In fact, it was reported that in the past ten years, $15 million dollars had been spent settling these complaints. This bill ensures that claims will be settled in 180 days or less and the funding would come from the involved Senators and Representatives. The settlements will also become public, instead of remaining a secret. In addition, these rights would now be given to interns, fellows, and other unpaid interns.

Elon Musk

On August 16th, Aretha Franklin, a singer songwriter passed away. She was also widely known as the Queen of Soul. Most people are familiar with her music but aren’t aware of the civil rights activism that she has participated in. She provided monetary donations to many civil rights groups. She would also perform at various benefits and protests to help raise awareness. Some of her songs are even based on this activism work that she’s done. For example, “Respect” and “Natural Woman” have become anthems for social change.

When she passed away, the official Black Lives Matter facebook posted about her passing. The post talks about how they appreciate her sharing her brilliance and helping so many people throughout her life. This shows that the community can stay strong through difficult times with things like music.

Bill Gates

On August 15th, 2015, Bill Gates, the founder of Microsoft, joined in and participated in the ice bucket challenge. He was challenged by Mark Zuckerberg and chose to nominate Elon Musk, Ryan Seacrest, and Chris Anderson. The video starts off with Bill watching Mark do the ice bucket challenge on a new Microsoft Surface device. He then says “I’m glad to give to ALS, it’s a great cause but I want to do it better than it’s been done.” He then shows designs for a contraption that will dump the ice bucket on his head when he pulls a rope. His video was particularly interesting because instead of simply pouring a bucket of ice water over his head, he built this machine that did it for him. This shows his internal creativity and extreme excitement for engineering and design. This personal touch made his video stand out and was part of the reason that this video received over 26 million views on Youtube.

Markus Persson

Markus is a Swedish programmer and video game designer. He is best known for starting the video game company Mojang which in turn created the popular sandbox building game, Minecraft. In his video, Markus attached a camera to his ice bucket. By doing this, his video gained a unique perspective. It looks like he had a lot of fun recording the video. He chose to nominate some other video game designers like Cliff Bleszinski, Bobby Kotick, and Phil Spencer.

Phil Spencer

Phil Spencer is the current vice-president of gaming at Microsoft. He is also the head of the Xbox brand, leading many different teams responsible for gaming at Microsoft. In his ice bucket challenge, Phil is wearing an Aperture Laboratories shirt which is from a popular video game called Portal. This shows his inner video game loving self and makes him seem very relatable and down to earth. Two Microsoft employees then pour a huge bucket of ice water onto his head. The video goes into slow motion to add dramatic effect. This works really well and helps the viewer feel how cold the water actually is. We can see ice flying out in slow motion and this really adds a unique spin to the typical ice bucket challenge video. Phil then says that he nominates Shuhei Yoshida from Sony, Reggie Fils-Aime from Nintendo and Gabe Newell from Valve to participate in the challenge.
